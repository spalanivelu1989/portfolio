# Brief — Personal Portfolio Site
**Version:** 1.0 · **Date:** 2026-05-20 · **Owner:** Senthil Palanivelu · **Status:** DRAFT — awaiting approval

---

## Problem
Senthil lacks a public-facing home on the web that demonstrates his technical ability to peers and the open-source community. Without it, there is no single place to point people toward his work.

## Audience
**Primary:** Developer peers and open-source community members who want to see what he builds and how he thinks.
**Secondary:** Recruiters or clients who land on the site after a referral or search.

## Core message
> "Here's what I can build — and how I build it."

Technical skills and shipped projects are the hero, not the résumé.

## Success criteria
- A visitor lands on the page and within 5 seconds understands who Senthil is and what he builds.
- The site is live and reachable at a public URL.
- All four v1 sections are present and functional.
- Passes Lighthouse performance ≥ 90, accessibility ≥ 85.

## V1 scope (must-have)
1. **Hero** — name, one-line tagline, primary CTA (e.g. "See my work")
2. **Projects** — curated list of past work with description, tech stack, and links (GitHub / live demo)
3. **About** — short bio: background, what you care about, how you work
4. **Contact** — email and key social links (GitHub, LinkedIn, etc.)

## Out of scope (v1)
- Blog or any long-form writing
- CMS or dynamic content management
- Analytics or visitor tracking
- Multilingual support

## Constraints
- **Stack:** Plain HTML, CSS, and vanilla JS — no framework, no build step.
- **Hosting:** Free tier only (GitHub Pages or Netlify).
- **Rule:** No untested code in production (per Constitution).

## Risks
- Content writing (bio, project descriptions) often blocks technical work — solve copy first.
- "Just one more section" scope creep — the Constitution rule is the guardrail.
